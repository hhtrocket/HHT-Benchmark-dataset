Research Context (Key Facts to Carry Forward)
==============================================

Direction S — Clinical Trial Recruitment Agent for Minority Underrepresentation

Core idea:
  Multi-agent AI system that identifies minority patients eligible for clinical trials
  and generates culturally-adapted, personalized outreach messages to reduce
  underrepresentation of Black, Hispanic, and other minority groups in clinical trials.

Three-agent architecture:
  Agent 1: EHR/SDOH extraction — parse clinical notes, extract SDOH factors
  Agent 2: Trial matching — match patient profiles to ClinicalTrials.gov eligibility criteria
  Agent 3: Outreach generation — produce personalized, culturally-adapted recruitment messages

Agent 3 scope (IMPORTANT):
  Addresses ONLY the information asymmetry layer (physician communication bias,
  lack of culturally-adapted messaging). Does NOT address structural economic
  barriers (insurance, transportation, income). Research hypothesis: AI nudging
  compensates for physician communication bias.

Why Gordon Gao will be interested:
  Gordon's JMIS 2025 paper on privacy-nudging field experiment directly connects
  to Agent 3's behavioral nudging mechanism. This gives the research a strong
  IS/management science framing that pure clinical informatics researchers would miss.

Why BIDS will be interested:
  Health equity focus, EHR NLP, OMOP CDM, AI safety in clinical deployment,
  real-world clinical data (MIMIC-IV) — all core BIDS research themes.

Evaluation strategy:
  Synthetic patient profile benchmark → Agent 3 generates outreach → LLM-as-judge
  evaluation validated by: multi-judge ensemble (GPT-4o + Claude + Gemini),
  CoT evaluation prompting, anchor examples, ICC against 20% human expert subset.
  Readability: Flesch-Kincaid score for health literacy appropriateness.

Key papers to follow:
  Clinical trial LLM matching: TrialGPT (2024), ANCORA (2024)
  Health equity AI: papers from JHU BIDS faculty on SDOH, disparities in ML
  LLM-as-judge: Zheng et al. MT-Bench (2023), CLEVER framework (healthcare),
                 EHR summarization ICC study (GPT-o3-mini, 2024)
